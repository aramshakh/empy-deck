## Design Context

### Users
Primary audience: 20–30 members of Aram’s mastermind group "Адаптация 2.0". Mixed AI maturity: some are advanced operators/builders, many are smart professionals who mostly use LLMs conversationally. Backgrounds include venture investing, law, psychiatry, esports, and software.

Context of use: live during a 2-hour kickoff session. The page is a visual speaking surface for a 45–60 minute talk, then a shareable recap link afterward.

Job to be done: help the speaker explain, in a sharp but accessible way, how to move from casual LLM usage to real AI leverage via workflows, agents, and vibe coding.

### Brand Personality
Calm, intelligent, sharp, contemporary.

Emotional goals:
- confidence without hype
- curiosity without intimidation
- clarity without oversimplification
- social credibility for a room full of strong people

Voice:
- concise
- adult
- culturally online but not cringe
- ambitious, but anti-bullshit

### Aesthetic Direction
Editorial-tech presentation page, not a generic startup landing page and not a corporate slide deck.

Visual tone:
- dark, cinematic, slightly intellectual
- strong typography first
- sparse but high-contrast composition
- minimal palette with one accent hue
- section-based storytelling with scroll rhythm

References in spirit:
- polished editorial microsites
- product keynote pages
- founder-quality one-page talk artifacts

Anti-references:
- generic SaaS card grids
- glowing AI slop gradients
- template deck aesthetics
- overloaded dashboards
- childish futurism

### Design Principles
1. Typography carries authority; decoration stays restrained.
2. Every section must deliver one idea cleanly.
3. The design should feel smarter than a standard slide deck but simpler than a product site.
4. Mixed-seniority audience: advanced enough for experts, legible enough for curious newcomers.
5. Keep the page credible, memorable, and easy to speak from live.
